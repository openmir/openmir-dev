In this game you will be asked to label an unknown clip of audio from the Openmir with a call from the Orcalab call catalog. The picture at the top is a button with a spectrogram image of the clip to match. Click it to play the sound. The buttons at the bottom are sounds from the Orcalab call catalog, you can click them to hear the sounds associated with them. The clips at the top of the screen have either been labelled by a scientist, or by a machine learning program that guessed which call it thought matched it best.

The aim of the game is to find the best matching sound for the clip at the top of the screen. When you've found a sound that, according to you, best matches it, click on the "Guess" button at the bottom of the screen. If you've labelled it the same as the scientist, you'll score a point. If not, don't be too concerned, it's possible that the scientist or the machine learning system just classified it incorrectly, and your guess will help the Openmir to learn and become better.

The classifications that you make are very valuable to our research, this is a task that is very hard for computers to do on their own, but with your labels, we can train the computer to eventually automatically find the orca calls in the vast archive of sound files we have in the Openmir.
